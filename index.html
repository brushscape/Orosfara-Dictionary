Suffixes Denote Parts of Speach

Ending Part of Speech
-a noun
-o verb
-i adjective
-e adverb
-u command

Flexible Word Order

Sek (Time Frame) ma (Subject) (Verb) tu (Indirect Object) ko (Direct Object).

Particle phrases must end in nouns. Adjectives/adverbs precede the nouns/verbs.

Example Sentences

The fish likes clean water.
Ma peixa paxo ko safi nita.

Quickly, catch the bug!
Sute hesu ko paka!

The rabbit wants to see a bird.
Ma kora kaf-xoko ko parina.

I walk to the store.
Ma yerena katito tir tukxaka.

Common Particles

Particle Usage
sek indicate the time-frame and/or tense of the verb
ma indicate a subject
o indicate an is/are/am verb equivalence; can also encompase "there is/are", "I have", etc.; takes the place of a verb
tu indicate an indirect object
ko indicate a direct object
tif indicate the location
hun indicate a dependent clause; use 'pen' conjugations to indicate what the clause is acting as/modifying
ki indicate a statement is a question
mi indicate a statement is a soft question; similar to adding "right?" or "isn't it?"
ni indicate a statement is a rhetorical question (optional)
ha indicate a statement is an exclamation (optional)

Example Sentences

I think I'm kind of cute.
Sikohun o kuxe suki.

You don't want to fight about this, do you?
Ma xerena nakaf-tantaro xut xeha, mi?

Did I give you the model yesterday?
Sek aktena keyo tu xerena ko moxa ka?

Come here right now!
Yeseke yexaku ha!

I can't remember what I was doing.
Nakap-memo kohun xanseke seyo ko kiya.

My friend heard you can eat and sleep here.
Sek xana ma yi tosa soto kohun kap-heko an nino tif yeha.
