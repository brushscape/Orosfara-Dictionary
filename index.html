Suffixes Denote Parts of Speach

Ending Part of Speech
-a noun
-o verb
-i adjective
-e adverb
-u command

Flexible Word Order

Tik (Time Frame) ma (Subject) (Verb) tu (Indirect Object) ko (Direct Object).

Particle phrases must end in nouns. Adjectives and adverbs precede the nouns.

Example Sentences

The fish likes clean water.
Ma peisa puno ko safi nita.

Quickly, catch the bug!
Soke hesu ko pika!

The rabbit wants to see a bird.
Ma kora kaf-fero ko parina.

I walk to the store.
Ma yerena katito ir tukjaka.

Common Particles

Particle Usage
ma indicate a subject
ko indicate a direct object
tu indicate an indirect object
o indicate an is/are/am verb equivalence; can also encompase "there is/are", "I have", etc.; takes the place of a verb
tik indicate the time-frame and/or tense of the verb
jak indicate the location
hun indicate a dependent clause; use 'pen' conjugations to indicate what the clause is acting as/modifying
ka indicate a statement is a question
amka indicate a statement is a soft question; similar to adding "right?" or "isn't it?"
naka indicate a statement is a rhetorical question (optional)
ha indicate a statement is an exclamation (optional)

Example Sentences

I think I'm kind of cute.
Siko kohun o kuje sukari.

You don't want to fight about this, do you?
Ma jerena nakaf-taruko jipun jeha, amka?

Did I give you the model yesterday?
Tik pertena keyo tu jerena ko mosa ka?

Come here right now!
Tik simsima yejaku ha!

I can't remember what I was doing.
Nakap-memo kohun tik pifa seyo ko kiya.

My friend heard you can eat and sleep here.
Tik pifa ma yi tosa fino kohun kap-heko an yano yejak.
